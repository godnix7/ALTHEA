Nov 18, 2025 • Journey
First-build jitters: how four students shipped Althea

We were nervous. It was our first time building something end‑to‑end and every decision felt massive. We sketched layouts, mapped flows, and researched APIs for weeks before choosing an approach that fit our budget and values. Balancing classes with late‑night sprints was rough—some days we met between lectures, other nights we barely slept. That pressure taught us discipline and focus. The goal stayed simple: a genuine, dependable space to talk that works even when other apps stumble.

Nov 12, 2025 • Engineering
Choosing LLaMA: practical AI for a student budget

We compared popular APIs and open models on cost, latency, safety, and control. LLaMA gave us the flexibility to tune prompts, add guardrails, and keep costs predictable. We layered context windows for journaling, added refusal rules for unsafe requests, and wrote evaluators to catch brittle replies. The result isn’t flashy; it’s careful. With transparent behavior and well‑scoped prompts, the bot listens first, responds gently, and escalates when signals cross our thresholds.

Nov 10, 2025 • Design
Colors that calm: our search for a soothing palette

We wanted the interface to make people feel safe, not hyped. We tested palettes against accessibility guidelines, checked contrast ratios, and read research on color psychology. Professor Nithin, who holds a PhD in psychology, helped us think about arousal, attention, and comfort. We landed on soft blues and warm neutrals, kept motion subtle, and used generous spacing to reduce cognitive load. The design fades into the background so the conversation can take center stage.

Nov 08, 2025 • Journey
Building under pressure: classes, labs, and long nights

We squeezed work into every spare hour—between labs, after exams, and during weekends. Some days we skipped lunch; a few times we even skipped class to unblock hard problems and align on architecture. Those sleepless nights taught us to plan weekly sprints, write crisp tasks, and check in honestly when we slipped. It wasn’t pretty, but it was real. That rhythm kept us moving and reminded us why we were doing this in the first place.

Nov 05, 2025 • Product
Better than “almost right”: learning from existing apps

We studied apps that promised support but stumbled on reliability, privacy, or tone. Some handled panic poorly; others buried settings or made escalation confusing. We wrote our own rules: clear copy, predictable paths, truthful responses, and a real escalation option when risk appears. The benchmark wasn’t hype—it was usefulness. By focusing on fewer, sturdier features, we avoided clever bugs and built a tool we’d trust during a tough night.
